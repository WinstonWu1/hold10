# Hold10 Legal And Safety Review Notes

Status: product safety checklist, not legal advice. Have qualified counsel review this before public App Store release.

## If Legal Review Is Deferred

Legal review is strongly recommended because Hold10 sits near sensitive areas: gambling urges, addiction support, mental health language, financial risk, crisis wording, and app store compliance. If legal review is deferred for the MVP, keep the release conservative:

- ship only local self-support features;
- avoid treatment, diagnosis, prevention, crisis response, monitoring, and guarantee claims everywhere;
- keep Help Now as a directory of third-party resources, not a live support promise;
- avoid accounts, analytics, cloud sync, peer community, coaching, risk scoring, contact notification, bank integrations, and gambling-blocking claims;
- complete every item in this checklist before submission;
- revisit legal review before expanding countries, adding data collection, or adding any higher-risk support feature.

## Current Product Boundary

Hold10 should be described as a self-support pause tool for gambling urges. It can support personal reflection, breathing timers, local progress tracking, Help Now links, self-exclusion prompts, and local data export/reset.

Hold10 should not be described as medical care, mental health care, therapy, gambling treatment, crisis counseling, legal advice, financial advice, debt counseling, monitoring, emergency response, betting, real-money gaming, lotteries, gambling tips, card counting, gambling facilitation, bank blocking, gambling account blocking, or guaranteed prevention.

## Required Disclaimer Coverage

- In-app footer says data is local and Hold10 is not medical, mental health, legal, or financial advice.
- Help Now screen says Hold10 is not monitored and is not an emergency, crisis, medical, mental health, gambling treatment, legal, or financial service.
- Privacy Policy says local recovery data can be sensitive and explains no monitoring or emergency response.
- Terms of Use say no professional advice, no emergency/crisis reliance, no monitoring, no guarantee, and no reliance for high-risk decisions.
- App Store review notes repeat the same safety boundary in plain language.

## Copy Rules

- Use: "self-support pause tool", "structured pause", "support resources", "local progress", "self-regulation".
- Avoid unless counsel approves: "treat", "cure", "prevent gambling disorder", "prevent addiction", "therapy", "clinical", "diagnose", "crisis response", "monitored", "guaranteed", "will stop gambling", "will prevent loss".
- Avoid entirely for the current MVP: betting, real-money gaming, lotteries, odds, gambling tips, card counting, gambling account access, or gambling facilitation.
- Any public claim about health, safety, performance, or outcomes must be truthful, specific, and supported.
- Money-protected values should be framed as user-entered estimates, not savings guarantees or financial records.

## Future Feature Triggers

Update legal copy, privacy labels, review notes, and release checklist before adding:

- accounts, cloud sync, analytics, crash reporting, payments, ads, email capture, or support messaging;
- coaching, peer community, live support, crisis escalation, contact notification, risk scoring, monitoring, reminders sent to others, or emergency workflows;
- bank integrations, gambling account integrations, gambling-blocking APIs, payment blocks, self-exclusion submission, or anything that claims to stop transactions;
- claims that Hold10 treats, prevents, mitigates, diagnoses, or cures gambling disorder, addiction, mental health issues, debt, or financial harm.

## Source Notes For Review

- Apple App Review Guidelines emphasize app safety, accurate metadata, honest age ratings, clear review notes, and careful handling of health, medical, personal, and sensitive data.
- Apple App Privacy Details require accurate disclosure of app and third-party data collection practices; on-device-only processing is treated differently from data transmitted off device.
- FTC health claim guidance emphasizes truthful, substantiated, non-misleading health-related claims.
- FTC mobile health app best practices emphasize minimizing unnecessary permissions and protecting sensitive data.
- FDA general wellness guidance draws a line around low-risk products intended for maintaining or encouraging a healthy lifestyle and unrelated to diagnosis, cure, mitigation, prevention, or treatment of disease or conditions.
- Apple's gambling guideline is relevant to positioning: Hold10 should stay clearly outside real-money gaming, lotteries, gambling aids, and gambling facilitation.
